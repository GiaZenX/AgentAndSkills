# Masterplan — <business name>

> One-line essence: what this back office automates, for whom.

The manager seeds this at onboarding and keeps it the living north star. PROCs are the log of
approved processes — do NOT mirror them here; the masterplan is redrawn only on an accepted change
of direction. Litmus test: a newcomer reading ONLY this file understands what the business is and
what the office team does for it.

## 1. Leitidee

A real paragraph: the business (what is sold, to whom, over which channels) and the automation
vision (what the user never wants to touch manually again; where they always stay in the loop).

## 2. Goals & non-goals

- Goals: e.g. "invoices land in the inbox and appear correctly filed + booked", "quarterly
  income/expense statement without manual collation", "product data becomes shop-ready copy".
- Non-goals (standing, from the constitution): no tax or legal advice, nothing is ever sent or
  published by the team, no live shop mutations in v1.

## 3. Processes to automate (the wish list — each becomes a PROC when approved)

| Wish | Trigger | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| invoice intake → filing + ledger | inbox drop | must |
| quarterly statement | quarter closed | must |
| product data → descriptions + supplier queries | inbox drop | … |
| compliance watch (categories × markets) | monthly / on request | … |
| marketing calendar + drafts | weekly / on request | … |

## 4. Boundaries & privacy

Jurisdictions/markets, Kleinunternehmer/USt situation (from business_profile), the user's
sensitive-document choice (process / redact / exclude) and the Claude account type — the honest
constraint that processed documents reach the Claude API under the user's account terms.

## 5. Risks & open questions

E.g. revenue-data sources still undefined (shop export? PSP export? bank statement?), scan quality
of incoming documents, supplier data completeness.

## 6. Own recommendations (manager's suggestions — clearly marked, max 1–3)

The team's ideas the user did not ask for, with honest effort/benefit.

## 7. Rollout outline

Preset path (core → commerce → full), which PROC goes live first, when the first quarterly report
is expected.
